Key findings

What is capping the offer, in order.

Distribution · Critical

A converting funnel starved of traffic

The funnel is ready to convert, but the top is thin: roughly 991 Instagram followers on about 14 posts, and a ~666-member free community, feeding a high-ticket program. The binding constraint is not the offer or the pages, it is the volume of qualified eyeballs entering them.

Fix: a real demand-gen engine, consistent content plus a paid-traffic path, feeding the opt-in you already have.
Proof · Critical

Placeholder testimonials on your Results page

The Results page still shows Lorem-ipsum testimonials with template names ("Amazing! - Peter S"), and the deal screenshots carry no names or dates. Selling a high-ticket program to a skeptical, peer-level audience, unfinished proof is the one visible hole that quietly costs conversions.

Fix: swap in named, verifiable client results with specifics; it is a fast, high-leverage change.
Content · High

Newest channel too thin to compound

You are active on Skool, YouTube and LinkedIn, but the Instagram presence, about 14 posts, is too light to build the authority and retargeting surface a $10k-plus offer needs. Low cadence on the channel where new buyers first check you out caps the funnel from the front.

Fix: a consistent content system on the channels that feed the offer, built for authority and retargeting.
Offer · Med

Two entry funnels, one message to split

There are two distinct paths, a direct free-training-to-call funnel and an apply-and-book funnel, with no visible pricing and no single obvious route. That can split traffic and dilute the message rather than concentrating it into one clean journey.

Fix: consolidate to one primary path and let the other serve a clear secondary intent.
The one-line takeaway

"Your funnel is genuinely built, so the fix is not more plumbing. It is volume into the top and finishing the proof: the Results page still runs lorem-ipsum testimonials, and about 14 posts cannot feed a high-ticket offer. Clean those two, then pour traffic in."
