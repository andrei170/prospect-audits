Key findings

What is costing the most, in order.

Owned assets · Critical

No owned lead-capture asset

Every Instagram lead flows into Smart City's funnel, which means the brokerage owns the contact, the database and the re-marketing rights. Build your own audience for years and you still walk away with nothing if you ever leave.

Fix: a personal one-page site and form (a Dallas neighborhood or rent-deal lead magnet) that captures the lead to your CRM before the hand-off.
Nurture · Critical

No follow-up turning renters into buyers

Renters convert on a timeline and many become first-time buyers in 12 to 24 months, which is the 2 to 3 percent commission upside. With no list or nurture tied to lease dates, that upside walks. You re-earn a small locating fee each time instead.

Fix: a simple CRM plus a renter-to-buyer nurture sequence timed to lease end dates.
Offer · High

Branded as a realtor, funnel built for locating only

The "realtor" positioning signals buyer and seller business, but there is no home-valuation capture, buyer or seller guide, or sold-listing proof to support it. The entire high-commission side of the brand has no funnel behind it.

Fix: a home-valuation landing page and buyer/seller guide lead magnets to open the higher-ticket side.
Reach · High

Instagram-only, no search capture

With no personal site there is zero "apartments in [Dallas neighborhood]" organic capture, so all reach depends on the algorithm and none on intent-driven search.

Fix: a few neighborhood landing pages and light local SEO to catch the renters actively searching.
The one-line takeaway

"You are sending every Instagram lead straight into Smart City's funnel. You get the commission once, but they keep the client. Capture those renters to your own list first, and you can turn them into buyers later."
