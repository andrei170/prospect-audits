The opportunity

The large majority of homeowners search online and read reviews before they hire a contractor. Right now, none of those people can find LIRD, so every one of them goes to a competitor who shows up. A website, a Google listing, and a simple way to capture and follow up leads would put LIRD in front of that demand for the first time, on top of the word-of-mouth work it already gets.

Key findings

What is costing the most, in order.

Website · Critical

No website, so no online front door

There is nowhere online for an interested homeowner to see the work, learn the services, or reach out after hours. Anyone who hears the name and looks it up finds nothing, and the trust and jobs go to whoever does have a site.

Fix: a clean, professional website with services, photos, and a quote request. (A demo has already been built for LIRD.)
Local search · Critical

Not on Google when people search

When a Long Island homeowner searches "drywall contractor near me," LIRD does not appear. A Google Business Profile is how local trades get found, get reviews, and show up on the map, and it is free.

Fix: set up and optimize a Google Business Profile, then build a few local pages so LIRD ranks for its towns.
Lead capture · Critical

No way to capture or follow up a lead

If someone cannot call at that moment, the lead is gone. There is no form, no place to leave details, and no system to follow up with the people who reach out, so interested homeowners quietly slip away.

Fix: a quote-request form that sends the lead straight to the phone, plus a simple follow-up habit or tool.
Reviews · High

No online reviews to build trust

Word of mouth is trust between two people. Online reviews are that same trust at scale, working around the clock. With none, a new customer has nothing to reassure them before they call.

Fix: a simple system to ask happy customers for a Google review after each job.
Branding · Strength

The brand is already a real asset

The logo, the "Hang, Tape, Finish" line, and the clean sign are genuinely good. That is a strong foundation. It just needs to live online where people can actually find it.

Fix: carry the existing brand straight onto the website and Google listing for a consistent, professional look.
