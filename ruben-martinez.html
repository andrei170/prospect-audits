The biggest opportunity is agents, not leads

Most brokers chase consumer leads. But for a broker, recruiting and keeping one productive agent is worth many times a single rental or buyer lead, and it compounds every year they stay. Right now "Join City Insight" is one link buried in a crowded Linktree with no page behind it. That is the highest-value gap on this whole scorecard, and the easiest to fix.

Key findings

What is costing the most, in order.

Recruiting · Critical

No funnel for the thing worth most to you

You run a brokerage, so agents are your compounding asset. Yet there is no dedicated page that sells an agent on joining you, no application flow, and no follow-up. It is one link competing with nine others. Every agent who ever considered you and did not act is revenue that never existed.

Fix: a dedicated agent-attraction funnel (page, application, follow-up) so recruiting runs as a system, not a link.
Conversion path · Critical

The link-in-bio asks visitors to do your sorting

Ten-plus buttons serving buyers, sellers, investors and agents all at once means every visitor has to figure out where they belong. Most will not bother. A crowded menu feels busy but converts worse than three clear doors.

Fix: cut to three clear paths (buy or sell, invest, join as an agent), each leading to a real page built to convert.
Capture · Critical

Guides with nothing behind them

The buyer and seller guides are good bait, but they are not tied to opt-in pages or a list, so people take the value and vanish. Nothing is captured, so nothing can be followed up or measured.

Fix: a proper opt-in landing page behind each guide, feeding a CRM you own.
Nurture · High

No follow-up on the leads you do get

Real estate rarely closes on first contact. Without an automated welcome and nurture sequence, the leads you work hard to create go cold while you are busy, and you re-earn every one from scratch.

Fix: automated email and text sequences per audience, so no lead is left to rot.
Brand · Strength

Your brand is genuinely an asset

The podcast, the personal brand, the brokerage, the content: that is real equity and it is why this is worth fixing. You are not starting from zero. You are sitting on attention with no system to catch it.

Fix: keep doing exactly what you are doing, and put a machine underneath it.
The one-line takeaway

"You have built a brand and a brokerage. But every person you send to your link lands in a ten-button menu with nothing behind it, and the one thing worth most to you, recruiting agents, is a buried link. Fix the paths, capture the leads, and build a real agent funnel, and the attention you already have starts paying you."
