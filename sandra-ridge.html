Key findings

What is costing the most, in order.

Owned assets · Critical

You own no digital asset

The whole online business sits on rented land: a Rendon Realty roster page, HAR and other portals, Instagram and Facebook, with a free Gmail as the only contact point. There is no personal website, no domain, and no email list. Everything you build strengthens someone else's platform, not yours.

Fix: a personal branded site and email domain that you own, with your record and listings hosted on it.
Lead capture · Critical

No way to capture or nurture a lead

There is no opt-in, no home-valuation tool, no home-search, and no list. Traffic you work hard to create lands in unstructured DMs and messages with no follow-up system, so most of it goes cold.

Fix: a "what's your home worth" or buyer-guide capture page feeding a simple CRM and a follow-up sequence.
Positioning · High

Three brands in one feed

The content blends residential real estate, a separate fix-and-flip investment brand, and heavy faith and community posting. All are genuine, but a prospect cannot tell in five seconds whether you help buyers, list sellers, or buy houses for cash. The mixed signal dilutes every one of them.

Fix: lead with one clear "who I help and how," and give the investment side its own lane.
Proof · Med

Real credibility, hidden from view

Eighteen years, a top-five-of-78 production record, and real active listings are genuine proof, but they live in an old magazine interview and a brokerage bio. On the assets you actually point people to, none of it is assembled or visible.

Fix: a results and reviews section on your own site that puts the track record front and center.
The one-line takeaway

"You have eighteen years and a top-five record, but your whole presence runs on a brokerage page, the MLS and a Gmail address. Own your own site and list, and you can finally capture the leads you already work so hard to create."
