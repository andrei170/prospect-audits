Key findings

What is costing the most, in order.

Proof · Critical

Placeholder testimonials and no real case studies

The homepage quotes are Lorem ipsum attributed to "John Doe" and "Jane Shaft," and the Design Library links to generic template previews rather than named client outcomes. For an agency whose promise is "websites that sell," having no verifiable results is the single biggest trust killer, exactly for the mid-size buyers you target.

Fix: replace placeholders with 3 to 5 real named results (before/after, lead lift, revenue) and turn the Design Library into a true case-study portfolio.
SEO · Critical

You sell SEO and content, but run neither for yourself

There is no blog and no content section anywhere on the site. Selling SEO while ranking on none of your own terms and publishing nothing is a credibility gap any informed prospect will spot.

Fix: stand up a local-SEO content hub that proves the capability you sell, and rank for your own service terms.
Offer · High

Hidden pricing and no productized offer

Pricing is nowhere, and the stack reads as a commodity "we do everything" generalist. The only path is a manual free consultation, which pushes all qualification onto a call.

Fix: define a productized flagship with a visible "starting at" and a clear systems framing to stand apart from generalists.
Funnel · High

Single conversion path, zero recapture

Every CTA is the same "free consultation." There is no lead magnet or email capture for the 95%+ who are not ready to book, so all non-ready traffic leaks with no way back.

Fix: add a low-friction lead magnet (a free website or SEO audit) plus an email nurture sequence.
The one-line takeaway

"You tell prospects you build websites that sell and that you do SEO and content, but your own site has placeholder testimonials, no case studies, and no blog. The proof engine you sell for clients is not running for you."
